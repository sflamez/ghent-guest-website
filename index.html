Discover Ghent

Cultural Highlights

Ghent is a city teeming with history and art. Don't miss the Gravensteen, a 10th-century castle with stunning city views. Marvel at the Adoration of the Mystic Lamb, the world-renowned altarpiece by the Van Eyck brothers in St. Bavo's Cathedral. For a modern twist, explore the street art that colors the city's alleys.

Shopping Guide

From high-end brands on the Veldstraat to unique boutiques in the smaller streets, Ghent offers a diverse shopping experience. Explore the Patershol neighborhood for artisan shops and galleries. On Sundays, enjoy the lively atmosphere of the Kouter flower market.

Food Recommendations

Breakfast & Coffee

  • Mokabon: A historic coffee roaster in the city center, perfect for high-quality coffee.
  • Bakkerij Klaas: Known for its delicious pastries and bread, ideal for a quick start.

Lunch

  • 't Klokhuys: Located in Patershol, offering traditional Flemish dishes like stoverij.
  • Beiruti: A great spot for delicious Lebanese & Middle Eastern cuisine.
  • De Appelier: A long-standing vegetarian restaurant with daily specials.

Dinner

  • Amadeus: Famous for its all-you-can-eat spare ribs. A local favorite!
  • Rumi: Offers flavorful and authentic Afghan cuisine in a cozy setting.
  • Publiek: For a special occasion, a Michelin-starred restaurant with an innovative menu.

Practical Information

Getting Around

Ghent has an excellent public transport system. Trams and buses are operated by De Lijn. You can buy tickets at vending machines at major stops, via their app, or from the driver. A day pass is often the most economical option for tourists.
